American Civil War

Start of the American Civil War

April 12, 1861
People: President Abraham Lincoln Confederate President Jefferson Davis
Cause: Secession of Southern states from the Union.
Effect: Began the conflict between the North and South.

Battle of Bull Run

July 21, 1861
People: General Irvin McDowell General Pierre G. T. Beauregard
Cause: Union forces attempted to seize control of Virginia.
Effect: Confederate victory boosted Southern morale.

Emancipation Proclamation

January 1, 1863
People: President Abraham Lincoln
Cause: To weaken the Confederacy by freeing slaves in rebellious states.
Effect: Declared all slaves in Confederate states to be free.

Battle of Gettysburg

July 1-3, 1863
People: General George Meade General Robert E. Lee
Cause: Confederate attempt to invade the North.
Effect: Union victory marked a turning point in the war.

Surrender at Appomattox Court House

April 9, 1865
People: General Ulysses S. Grant General Robert E. Lee
Cause: Confederate forces were overwhelmed by Union forces.
Effect: Led to the end of the Civil War.

Assassination of Abraham Lincoln

April 14, 1865
People: President Abraham Lincoln John Wilkes Booth
Cause: Booth's opposition to Lincoln's policies.
Effect: Lincoln's assassination shocked the nation and led to political turmoil.
