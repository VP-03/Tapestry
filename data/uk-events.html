War of the Roses

First Battle of St Albans

22 May 1455
People: Richard, Duke of York King Henry VI Edmund Beaufort
Cause: Conflicts between the House of York and the House of Lancaster over the English throne.
Effect: Yorkist victory, marking the beginning of the Wars of the Roses.

Battle of Wakefield

30 December 1460
People: Richard, Duke of York Margaret of Anjou Henry VI
Cause: Yorkist challenge to Lancastrian rule.
Effect: Lancastrian victory; Richard, Duke of York, was killed, and his son Edward IV was declared the new Yorkist leader.

Battle of Towton

29 March 1461
People: Edward IV Henry VI Richard Neville
Cause: Edward IV's attempt to consolidate his rule.
Effect: Yorkist victory, leading to Edward IV's coronation as king of England.

Battle of Barnet

14 April 1471
People: Edward IV Richard Neville Margaret of Anjou
Cause: Lancastrian attempt to regain the throne.
Effect: Yorkist victory, leading to the death of Edward, Prince of Wales, and the capture and execution of Henry VI.

Battle of Tewkesbury

4 May 1471
People: Edward IV Richard Neville Margaret of Anjou
Cause: Lancastrian attempt to regain the throne.
Effect: Yorkist victory, leading to the death of Edward, Prince of Wales, and the capture and execution of Henry VI.
